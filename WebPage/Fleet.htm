Seneca Area Emergency Services

Seneca maintains a fleet of emergency vehicles to serve our communities.  We have attempted to give a brief overview of equipment carried by each unit.

 

ALS Medic Unit 161

 

Manufactured by Horton this 2003 unit serves primarily the Etna and Sharpsburg communities.  It is staffed by an EMT and Paramedic and is the primary unit for first call duties.

                                            Equipment:    -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    12 Lead Cardiac Monitoring

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

                                                                    -    Light Rescue Equipment

                                                                 

ALS Medic Unit 162

Manufactured by Road Rescue Ambulance this unit serves primarily the Etna and Sharpsburg communities.  It is staffed by an EMT and Paramedic and is the primary 2nd out unit.

                                            Equipment:    -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    12 Lead Cardiac Monitoring

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

 

 

ALS Medic Unit 166

This unit serves primarily Indiana Township.  It is staffed by an EMT and Paramedic and is the designated first out unit for Indiana Township

                                                Equipment -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    12 Lead Cardiac Monitoring

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

                                                                    -    Fire Department Support Equipment

 

ALS Squad 164

A 1996 Chevy blazer the unit serves all of our communities with additional ALS personnel.  It is staffed by a Paramedic and when needed an EMT.   The unit runs first out on any priority 1 dispatch and to assist BLS crews when our schedule makes a tiered response necessary.

                                            Equipment:     -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    12 Lead Cardiac Monitoring

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

                                                                    -    Fire Department Support Equipment

                                                                    -    Incident Command and Mass Care Equipment

 

Seneca 168 Supervisor Vehicle

 

This former police vehicle donated to Seneca by the Borough of Sharpsburg is used by the on duty supervisor.  It responds to multiple patient incidents, structure fires, Priority 1 calls, and most mutual aid dispatches.  It responds to all of our communities and allows field supervision of our crews as well as back up for difficult or complicated calls.

                                            Equipment:    -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    Communications Equipment

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

                                                                    -    Incident Command and Mass Care Equipment

 

Seneca 169 Chiefs Vehicle

This 2003 Ford Expedition  is used by the on call Director.  It responds to multiple patient incidents, structure fires, Priority 1 calls, and  mutual aid dispatches.  It responds to all of our communities and allows field supervision of our crews as well as back up for difficult or complicated calls.

                                             Equipment:    -    Basic and Advanced Life Support Equipment

                                                                    -    Communications Equipment

                                                                    -    Forcible Entry Equipment

                                                                    -    Incident Command and Mass Care Equipment
