Respiratory Distress: Pulmonary Edema, CHF

Criteria:     Shortness of breath with any of the following:

·        bilateral rales

·        history of congestive heart failure

·        wheezing with cardiac history or symptoms.

 

 

Interventions:

 

1.       Assure adequate airway; suction, ventilate and intubate as needed.

Note:  Intubation should be initially reserved for patients with decreased mental status or impending respiratory arrest.  Otherwise, aggressive medical management should be trialed prior to intubation.

 

2.      Oxygen 15 L/min via non-rebreather mask

 

3.      Monitor ECG and determine rhythm (Use applicable arrhythmia protocol.)

 

4.      Nitroglycerin SL as follows:

·        SBP > 140:     0.8 mg every 3-5 min

·        SBP 101-140:  0.4 mg every 3-5 min

·        SBP 70-100 without signs/symptoms of shock:

Contact Command to consider Dobutamine infusion (5-20 mcg/kg/min)

·        SBP < 70 or SBP 70 - 100 with signs/symptoms of shock:

Contact Command to consider Dopamine infusion (5-20 mcg/kg/min)

 

5.      IV saline lock or IV KVO if saline lock unavailable

 

6.      If peripheral edema present: Lasix 40 mg (or double oral dose, up to 120mg) IV

 

7.      If wheezing present: Albuterol 2.5 mg/3 cc NSS via nebulization

 

Contact Medical Command

 

8.      Consider additional Nitroglycerin, Lasix and Albuterol

 

9.      Consider Morphine Sulfate in doses of 2 - 4 mg IV (5 - 10 mg pre-intubation)
