Incident Response

705 Main Street, Sharpsburg PA

Structure Fire with Entrapment

 

On Thursday Morning January 25, 2001 at 12:55 AM Seneca units along with Sharpsburg Fire Department Station 265 and Aspinwall Fire Department Station 102 were dispatched to 705 Main Street an 11 unit apartment building.  First arriving  units were confronted with multiple victims attempting escape from a 3 story structure with heavy fire involvement concentrated in the rear 50% of the building.  As crews initiated rescue operations and called for additional mutual aid companies, EMS units set up incident command and triage areas. 

Initial rescue efforts by truck companies and emergency services personnel on scene saved 5 victims from windows and rooftops as the fire quickly spread through the building.  Rescue efforts for additional trapped residents were hampered by severe heat and smoke conditions and during the rescue operations 3 firefighters and a police officer who sustained injuries caused by the collapse of an extension ladder.

EMS  personnel quickly called for additional ambulances and instituted their MCI plan.  Overall command was assumed by Brian Dankis, Assistant Director (502) who initiated Triage and the accountability of residents.  Throughout the incident 11 patients were transported to the hospital, 9 Patients were treated and released on scene, and rehab was provided to firefighters engaged in the operation.  Tragically, there were a total of 4 fatalities recovered from the fire scene over the next 3 days.

By the end of the operation 3 days later it was  evident that the incident had deeply impacted the individuals involved, the service as a whole, and the community of Sharpsburg.  Our personnel were involved along with the fire departments for 3 straight days with the initial fire attack, subsequent rekindles, and recovery operations.  With the number of patients crews were initially confronted with we could have never made the system work without our mutual aid services.  On behalf of our service, the Fire Department, and the community we would like to thank all that participated in the efforts listed below.  We would especially like to thank the Fire Marshall's office for involving the departments in subsequent operations and allowing some sense of closure to the firefighters who initially responded.
